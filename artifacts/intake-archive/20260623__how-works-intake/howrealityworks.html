Core statement

Reality, meaning, and collapse

v0.1
Thesis: Reality can be modeled as a globally recursive invariant with no base case, while meaning within reality consists of locally recursive semantic loops that may or may not collapse depending on witness conditions.
Let R be the global closure (no outside reference).
Define an operator F over descriptions of “what holds.”

Global: R is a fixed point: F(R) = R   (no base case required).
Local: a semantic loop ℓ remains undecided until witnessed.

Witness w supplies an interpretation within a confidence window.
Collapse is partial, situated, and may fail (drift/decay) if unwitnessed too long.

Therefore: we can measure gradients (effects), not an external “source.”
What this page refuses to do

It refuses to treat “reality” as an object that needs an origin story. It refuses to turn “meaning” into a property that exists without witnessing. It refuses to conflate record-keeping with interpretation. It refuses to promise universal collapse.

Mode: pointer (non-final) Tip: linger. don’t force a base case.
Local dynamics

Semantic loops (situated)

SCT-aligned
Loop = time-bound exchange carrying intention or rhythm. It can remain undecided.
Collapse = when the loop is witnessed and meaning is fixed—privately, shared, or publicly.
Entropy = the weight of uncollapsed loops (unfinalized meaning) that may drift or decay.
Given loops {ℓᵢ}:
SE ≈ Σ (ℓᵢ · (1 - cᵢ))
where cᵢ is witness confidence that meaning has been assigned.

When cᵢ → 1, that loop contributes ~0 semantic entropy.
When cᵢ stays low and time passes, drift increases; collapse can fail.
Witness conditions (minimal)

A valid witness does more than record: it interprets. Timing matters (confidence window). Different witnesses differ (bias as structure). Some loops never collapse—and that’s not “error,” it’s design space.

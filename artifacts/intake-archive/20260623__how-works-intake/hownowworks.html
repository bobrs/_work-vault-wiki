The How

1
No narration

Notice

Not analysis. Not labels. Just: something is happening. If you can feel it in the body, you’re on the wave.

2
Create options

Soften

Soften is not collapse. It’s unclenching enough to steer: longer exhale, jaw unhooked, shoulders down a few percent.

3
Reversible

Respond

Make a small move you can undo: one honest question, one step, one pause before certainty. Let reality answer back.

What this is

A micro-loop for staying oriented when the moment is real. It works because it’s self-updating: your response changes the situation, and the situation gives new information.

What this is not

Not positivity. Not suppression. Not “make it okay.” Not control. It’s participation with enough looseness to learn.

Anti-How (signals you left the wave)

  • Rushing to conclude
  • Freezing to be safe
  • Forcing agreement
  • Seeking purity or certainty
  • Trying to “win the moment”

When you notice these, you don’t need shame—just restart at Step 1.

30-second reset

1) Name the fact: “This is happening.” 2) Exhale longer than inhale (x3). 3) Choose the smallest reversible move. 4) Let reality respond. Repeat.

FAQ

Is this meditation?

It can include calm, but it’s not a retreat from action. It’s an orientation loop you can use during life—mid-conversation, mid-decision, mid-chaos.

What if the situation is serious?

Then the loop matters more. “Act small” doesn’t mean “do nothing.” It means: pick the smallest move that keeps options alive and improves information.

What if I keep failing at it?

That’s normal. Wipeouts are data. The skill is restarting without self-attack: Notice → Soften → Respond.

How does this relate to expectations?

Expectations explain how we get disoriented. How Now Works is what you do when the map stops matching the territory: regain stance, preserve variance, continue learning.

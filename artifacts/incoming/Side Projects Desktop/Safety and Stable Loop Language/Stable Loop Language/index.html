The core idea, in one glance

Most “I want to feel X” sentences secretly bundle together three different things: who I want to be, what I’d like you to do, and the kind of space I want us to share. Stable Loop Language simply says: don’t compress those. Name them.

1 · Identity

“I want to be someone who shows my value clearly.”

You take responsibility for who you’re becoming, instead of asking others to manage your feelings.

2 · Behavior

“I respond well when you tell me what you appreciate in concrete terms.”

You request specific, observable actions instead of vague emotional outcomes.

3 · Context

“Can we create a dynamic where appreciation is spoken out loud regularly?”

You co-design the environment so good feelings emerge naturally, without pressure.

## Working Read

The spine is the structural explanation layer: primitives, composites, and the way the system is assembled.

It is the place where the project names its comparators and shows how one invariant becomes a relation, a repair move, or a domain translation.

## Core Claim

The spine is the project's internal grammar. It tells you which pieces are primitive, which are composite, and how the system stays legible when those pieces are mapped into different domains.

## Mechanisms

- Define primitives before applications.
- Treat composites as stable relations rather than ad hoc examples.
- Preserve a repair path when a boundary, comparator, or feedback loop changes.
- Keep translation into domains anchored to the same underlying primitives.
